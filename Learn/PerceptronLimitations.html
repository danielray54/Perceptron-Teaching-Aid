The XOR Problem

Perceptron's are very limited in their abilities. A perceptron can only recognise patterns that a linearly seperable, meaning that the data points being used to train the perceptron can only be seperated by a line. To demonstrate this point, we can look at logical gates and how the perceptron can be used or not used to recognise their patterns.
For both the AND and OR logical gates, the data points can be seperated by a single line, thus making them linearly seperable problems. However, the issue arises when we look at the XOR or Exclusive Or logical gate, as this gate is linearly inseperable. Pima Indians Diabetes Data Set

To further demonstrate the limitations of the Perceptron, we can apply real world data to the Perceptron to see where the limitations are.
Pima Indians Diabetes dataset is originally from the National Institute of Diabetes and Digestive and Kidney Diseases. The objective of the dataset is to diagnostically predict whether or not a patient has diabetes, based on certain diagnostic measurements included in the dataset. Similar to the Iris Data Set, the data can be classified into the classes, 0 or 1 - which in this case gives an outcome of likelihood of getting diabetes within the next 5 year. The data set differs from the Iris data set by having 8 variables instead of 4, look at a sample of the data below:
